‚öîÔ∏è Gameplay

Game Objective

Reduce your opponent's health from 20 to 0 by attacking with your units while defending against their attacks. The last player standing wins!

Turn Structure

üîÑ
Turn Phase Diagram
Image: turn-phase-diagram.png - Visual flowchart showing all game phases

Each turn consists of the following phases:

1. Rally Phase üé∫

The start of your turn. You gain mana equal to your turn number (Turn 1 = +1 mana, Turn 2 = +2 mana, up to +10 mana per turn).

üíé
Mana Crystals Display
Image: mana-display.png - Visual representation of available mana
  • Mana accumulates - Save it for expensive units!
  • All your units become ready (exhaustion removed)
  • Click "DRAW CARD" to proceed to the next phase

2. Draw Phase üé¥

Draw one card from your deck to add to your hand.

‚úã
Hand Display
Image: hand-display.png - Player's hand showing 7 cards
  • Starting hand: 7 cards
  • Maximum hand size: 8 cards
  • If hand exceeds 8 cards, you must discard at end of turn

3. Deploy Phase (First) üéñÔ∏è

Deploy units from your hand to your reserves (battlefield).

‚öîÔ∏è
Deploy Phase Interface
Image: deploy-phase.png - Deploying units from hand to battlefield
  • Click cards in your hand to deploy them
  • Each card costs mana equal to: Attack + Defense - 1
  • Maximum 7 units in your reserves at once
  • Newly deployed units are exhausted (cannot attack this turn)
  • Click "END DEPLOY" when finished

‚ö†Ô∏è Important

If your reserves are full (7/7 units), the deploy phase is automatically skipped!

4. Attack Phase ‚öîÔ∏è

Attack your opponent with ready units (units that aren't exhausted).

üó°Ô∏è
Attack Phase
Image: attack-phase.png - Selecting units to attack
  • Click "READY" on units to mark them as attackers
  • Units with 0 attack (walls) cannot attack
  • Attacking exhausts the unit
  • You can attack multiple times with different units
  • Click "END ATTACK" when finished

5. Blocker Phase üõ°Ô∏è

If your opponent attacks, you may block with your units.

üõ°Ô∏è
Blocking Interface
Image: blocking-phase.png - Selecting blockers to defend against attack
  • Click up to 2 units to block the attack
  • Exhausted units CAN block (defense never exhausts)
  • Walls (0 attack) make excellent blockers
  • Click "FINISHED" to resolve combat

6. Combat Resolution ‚öîÔ∏èüõ°Ô∏è

Combat is resolved automatically:

üí•
Combat Resolution
Image: combat-resolution.png - Damage calculation and card destruction
  • Attacker deals damage equal to its attack to blockers
  • Blockers deal damage equal to their combined attack to the attacker
  • Units take damage equal to their defense
  • Destroyed units are sent to the discard pile
  • Unblocked damage hits the defending player's health

üí° Combat Example

A 3/2 attacker attacks. Opponent blocks with 0/3 and 2/1 units:

  • Attacker (3 attack) deals 3 damage total to blockers
  • Blockers (2 combined attack) deal 2 damage to attacker
  • Attacker takes 2 damage (2 defense) - SURVIVES
  • 0/3 blocker takes 3 damage (3 defense) - DESTROYED
  • 2/1 blocker survives (no damage remaining)

7. Deploy Phase (Second) üéñÔ∏è

After combat, you get another chance to deploy units with any remaining mana. Same rules as the first deploy phase.

8. Discard Phase üóëÔ∏è

If you have more than 8 cards in hand, you must discard down to 8.

üóëÔ∏è
Discard Interface
Image: discard-phase.png - Selecting cards to discard from hand
  • Click cards to inspect them
  • Click "DISCARD" button to discard selected card
  • Repeat until hand size is 8 or fewer

9. End Turn üîÑ

Turn automatically ends after discard phase. Your opponent's turn begins with their Rally Phase.

üé¥ Card Mechanics

Understanding Cards

üÉè
Card Anatomy
Image: card-anatomy.png - Labeled card showing attack, defense, and cost

Each unit card has three key stats:

Stat Symbol Description
Attack ‚öîÔ∏è Damage dealt during combat (0-6). Cards with 0 attack are "walls" and cannot attack.
Defense üõ°Ô∏è Health/toughness of the unit (1-6). When defense reaches 0, the unit is destroyed.
Cost üíé Mana required to deploy (calculated as Attack + Defense - 1). Ranges from 0 to 11.

Special Cards

Mythic Cards (6/6)

The most powerful cards in the game:

  • Stats: 6 attack, 6 defense (cost 11)
  • Limit: Only 1 per deck
  • Power: Can attack and defend effectively
  • Strategy: Save for critical moments!

Walls (0 attack)

Defensive units that excel at blocking:

  • Cannot attack (0 attack value)
  • High defense for absorbing damage
  • Ideal blockers - protect your health
  • Cost-efficient defense

Combat Details

Damage Calculation

Combat follows these rules:

  1. Attacker's damage: Equal to its attack value
  2. Blocker's damage: Equal to combined attack of all blockers
  3. Damage is dealt simultaneously
  4. Units are destroyed when damage ‚â• defense
  5. Surviving damage: Units can be damaged but not destroyed (defense > damage)

‚ö†Ô∏è Blocking Strategy

You can block with up to 2 units. Choose wisely:

  • Use walls to absorb damage without losing attackers
  • Combine blockers to destroy the attacker
  • Sometimes it's better to take damage than lose valuable units

Unblocked Damage

If you choose not to block (or cannot block), you take damage equal to the attacker's attack value directly to your health.

‚ù§Ô∏è
Health Display
Image: health-display.png - Life total counter showing current health

Advanced Mechanics

Multiple Blockers

When blocking with 2 units:

  • Their defense values combine to absorb damage
  • Their attack values combine to damage the attacker
  • Damage is distributed (first blocker takes damage until destroyed, then second blocker)

Multiple Attacks

You can attack multiple times per turn:

  • Attack with one unit ‚Üí Resolve combat ‚Üí Attack with another
  • Each attack is resolved separately
  • Opponent blocks each attack individually
  • Strategic advantage: Force opponent to use multiple blockers

üéØ Strategy Guide

Basic Strategy Concepts

Resource Management

Mana is your most precious resource:

  • Spend wisely: Don't deploy everything at once
  • Save for threats: Keep mana for responding to opponent's plays
  • Mana grows each turn: You'll have more resources in late game
  • Card advantage matters: Trading 1 card to destroy 2 is excellent

When to Attack

  • Opponent has no blockers: Free damage!
  • Favorable trades: Your unit survives, theirs doesn't
  • Pressure opponent: Force them to block with valuable units
  • Lethal opportunity: Finishing blow to win the game

When to Block

  • Survival: Block if the damage would kill you
  • Favorable trades: Kill their attacker with your blocker
  • Protect life total: Health is a resource - don't waste it
  • Use walls effectively: Walls are great blockers (high defense, 0 attack)

Deck Building Strategy

Mana Curve Principles

üìà
Ideal Mana Curve
Image: ideal-mana-curve.png - Graph showing optimal card distribution by cost

The mana curve is the distribution of card costs in your deck. A good curve ensures you have plays at every stage of the game:

  • Early game (0-2 cost): 6-10 cards - Ensure you have plays in early turns
  • Mid game (3-5 cost): 12-18 cards - The core of most decks
  • Late game (6-8 cost): 8-14 cards - Powerful finishers
  • Very late (9-11 cost): ‚â§6 cards - Don't include too many expensive cards

‚ö° Aggro (Rush)

Average Cost: <3.5

Strengths:

  • Fast wins
  • Strong early game
  • High pressure

Weaknesses:

  • Runs out of steam
  • Weak late game
  • Vulnerable to walls

‚öñÔ∏è Midrange

Average Cost: 4.5-5.5

Strategy: Balanced approach with efficient units at all stages.

Strengths:

  • Versatile
  • Adapts to opponent
  • Consistent plays

Weaknesses:

  • No clear advantage
  • Can be outpaced by aggro
  • Can be outvalued by control

üè∞ Control (Late Game)

Average Cost: >6.0

Strategy: Survive early game, dominate with powerful late-game cards.

Strengths:

  • Powerful cards
  • Strong late game
  • Card quality advantage

Weaknesses:

  • Slow start
  • Vulnerable early
  • Needs walls/blockers

Advanced Tips

Card Economy

Value your cards based on their stats:

  • Card value = Attack + Defense (approximately)
  • Don't sacrifice a 5/5 (value 10) to kill a 1/1 (value 2)
  • Look for favorable trades where you gain more value than you lose
  • Walls have high defensive value but low attacking value

Tempo vs Value

  • Tempo: Speed and pressure (aggro decks prioritize this)
  • Value: Card quality and efficiency (control decks prioritize this)
  • Balance both to create a strong deck

The Mythic Card

Every deck has exactly one 6/6 mythic card:

  • Most powerful unit in your deck
  • High cost (11 mana) - Save mana for it
  • Game-winning potential - Can attack and block effectively
  • Don't waste it: Use strategically for maximum impact

Playing Against Mr. Bones (AI)

ü§ñ
Mr. Bones - AI Opponent
Image: mr-bones-ai-opponent.png - The AI opponent character

Mr. Bones is an intelligent AI opponent with sophisticated decision-making:

AI Behavior

  • Survival priority: Will always block if it would die
  • Health-aware: Blocks more aggressively at low health
  • Value-based trades: Won't sacrifice expensive units for cheap attackers
  • Attacker elimination: Prefers to kill attackers when economical
  • Cost optimization: Uses cheapest blocking combination

Exploiting AI Weaknesses

  • Small repeated attacks: AI may let small damage through to preserve units
  • Cheap attackers: AI won't waste valuable blockers on cheap threats
  • Predictable blocking: AI follows mathematical optimization

üîß Troubleshooting

Common Issues

Problem: Cards not appearing in hand

Solution:

  • Ensure you clicked "DRAW CARD" during the draw phase
  • Check that your deck was properly loaded (look for confirmation message)
  • If using custom deck, ensure it validated successfully
  • Try standing up and sitting down again to reset the game

Problem: Can't deploy cards

Solution:

  • Check you have enough mana (shown in mana crystal display)
  • Verify reserves aren't full (maximum 7 units)
  • Ensure it's your turn (check turn indicator)
  • Make sure you're in the Deploy Phase

Problem: Can't attack with units

Solution:

  • Units must be ready (not exhausted) to attack
  • Newly deployed units cannot attack until next turn
  • Units with 0 attack (walls) cannot attack
  • Ensure it's the Attack Phase

Problem: Deck Builder HUD not appearing

Solution:

  • Right-click the HUD and select "Detach"
  • Right-click again and select "Wear"
  • Ensure it's attached to the correct attachment point
  • Check that HUD hasn't auto-deleted (attach within 30 seconds of rezzing)

Problem: Custom deck won't load

Solution:

  • Ensure you're seated at the game table (security requirement)
  • Check your internet connection (cloud deck fetching requires connectivity)
  • Verify deck is valid (check for ‚úì on Deck List)
  • Try clicking SELECT again after a few seconds

Problem: "Max 5 decks reached" error

Solution:

  • You can only have 5 decks stored at a time
  • Delete an old deck before creating a new one
  • Use EXPORT to save deck data before deleting if you want to keep a backup

Problem: Game messages are overwhelming

Solution:

  • Use verbosity commands to control message amount
  • Type /1 verbosity normal for less text
  • Type /1 verbosity silent for minimal messages
  • Type /1 help to see all verbosity options

Deck Builder Issues

Problem: Cards showing as gray boxes

Solution: Card textures are missing from HUD inventory. Contact the HUD creator or administrator.

Problem: Can't save deck

Solution:

  • Ensure deck has a name (not default prompt text)
  • Ensure color is selected
  • Check internet connection
  • Verify you haven't reached 5-deck limit

Problem: Deck validation failed

Solution:

  • Click "ANALYZE" to see specific problems
  • Check deck has exactly 40 cards
  • Verify no card appears more than 4 times (except mythic/cost-11)
  • Ensure only one 6/6 card in deck

Performance Tips

  • Detach HUD when not in use - Saves region resources
  • Don't spam buttons - Wait for confirmations before clicking again
  • Use ANALYZE before gameplay - Catch deck problems early
  • Keep graphics settings reasonable - Medium or higher for best experience

Getting Help
